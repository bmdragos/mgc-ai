O2 Cell Aging — Stable Then Steep

Sep 2019 – Mar 2026 • Full Meridian history • One complete lifecycle validated

Full Meridian history (Sep 2019 – Mar 2026): Three O2 cell lifecycles visible. The cleanest example is Apr 2021 – Oct 2023: gain climbed from 14 → 31 over 30 months. The aging pattern is “stable then steep” — flat for ~18 months (the 1st derivative near zero), then the knee point where both drift rate and acceleration become consistently positive. From the knee point, a Taylor expansion g(t+Δt) ≈ g + g′·Δt + ½g″·Δt² predicts the soft limit crossing to within 2–4 weeks, displayed as a running median of weekly predictions. Fresh cells start at gain ~14–15, the soft limit is 28.2.

Device 2 (SN 253000059): Current cell gain stable at ~18.8. The March 2026 phase delay warnings were entirely caused by umbilical flow restriction, not cell degradation.
